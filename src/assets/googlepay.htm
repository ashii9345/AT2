Google Pay: Adding a favorite bar.

Tarun Kumar
5 min read
·
Sep 28, 2023

--

Helping the user by adding a favorite bar to make payments more efficient and less time consuming.

Why I started this design challenge:

Being in a Creative field, I’ve started my journey as a Graphic Designer and Video Editor. Creative Design and the ideas behind it have always intrigued me. My exposure to Graphic Design has always been through Google and YouTube. It took me almost a year to decide to get into UX design course. Finally, I thought of giving it a deep dive. After Contacting many other UX courses and looking through the Internet, I landed on UX Anudeep YouTube Page. I found the way of communication and the way of explaining concepts very interesting. I decided Growth school was my source through which I could explore the world of UX.

My initial experience of the Career Accelerator Program was pretty exciting. The #StudentforLife has now become my motto thanks to Anudeep. In the 2 weeks of Level 0 of this course I’ve learned all the root concepts that go into any App or its Desing. The craving for more knowledge has already started in me.

So as a part of this program, we were tasked with identifying a problem we faced with an app we use daily. I took on this opportunity to put my Skills and my learning together and chose to work on Google Pay app and improve its users time consumption and make their daily task easy by adding a favorites bar.

My final redesign of Google Pay’s Favorite bar:

Here is the comparison of the original screen and the Redesign.

What is the problem, its solution and how it helps the users?

Many of them use Google Pay daily in order to send money. Google Pay shows the whole list of people. It is a problem to go through the whole list of people. There is not a quick pay thing inside the app.

To address this issue, I came up with an idea to add a Favorites bar that the users can add their favorite persons to whom they can send money immediately. This works when user makes a long press on one of their friends/cousins/vendors and then drags and drops them inside the favorites bar. This gives the users a more flexibility over the app by customizing the people by their preference.

What business metrics does it affect?

The Favorite bar is an option which increases the user’s control and makes their tasks easy and convenient, therefore reducing the sessions length.

Why did I select this App and how I reached the final solution?

After going through the workbooks “Learning from Existing design” and “Business Impact of UX Design” I looked into many apps that I use on a daily basis and came across Google Pay. Now a days UPI being a most common mode of payments and most of them using Google Pay I came across this problem when I wanted to pay money to one of my friends. The problem I identified was that there is not much customization in this app and came up with this solution of adding a favorites bar which brings user flexibility.

Paper/Low-fidelity Prototype:

I was very excited to implement my idea on a paper before going on with the idea. This helped me finalizing how my idea resonates on the screen.

Then creating a prototype with these low fidelity prototypes and using the Marvel App to make an onscreen display.

Making High-fidelity prototype (UI screen):

By applying my knowledge of the Designing and then I started creating a High-fidelity prototype by using Figma.

I used all the lessons that I learned from the Cohort and applied them onto creating the design.

It is a pretty simple yet effective solution of adding a favorites bar. This option is going to be pretty useful for users.

Validating if my solutions will work through Usability testing:

After completing the Design and the prototype, it was the time to test my design and know through the user if my solution works or not, or if there is any change needed in it.

I made a list of simple tasks for my users and see their feedback.

Learnings or Feedback from the users:

After the tasks were completed by the user it was now the time to take observation and record any feedback if given.

Received positive feedback from the users with the solution presented and received feedback that I should be making the favorite bar users more visible by adding a star icon on them.

Improving my Designs after Usability Testing:

Taking feedback from the users and creating a design to blend the with my previous design. So, I added a yellow star icon on top of all those users that were added in the favorites bar.

Key Learnings:

I found it quite interesting that you can be even more specific by adding both visual and verbal indication.

Gained a lot of knowledge and confidence of how much I did on my first testing. This way I found out my pros and cons by directly jumping into the case study. I find this way of learning things way more interesting. Thanks to Anudeep for choosing to make us learn and achieve some sense of confidence by this method. I was not sure if I could do it in the first place, now I am getting ready for more challenges.
